# Privacy Policy - Rainy Cowork

**Last Updated: January 26, 2026**

## 1. Introduction

Welcome to Rainy Cowork's Privacy Policy. This policy explains how we handle your personal information when you use our open-source AI desktop agent. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you maintain control over your data, in alignment with Enosis Labs' principles of digital sovereignty.

## 2. Our Privacy Philosophy

### 2.1 Digital Sovereignty
We believe that data control is a fundamental right. Rainy Cowork is designed with privacy-first principles:
- **Local Processing**: Your data stays on your device whenever possible
- **User Control**: You decide what data to share and with whom
- **Transparency**: Open-source code allows full audit of our practices
- **Minimal Collection**: We collect only what's necessary for functionality

### 2.2 Alignment with Enosis Labs
Our privacy practices align with [Enosis Labs' Privacy Policy](https://enosislabs.vercel.app/privacy) and their commitment to responsible AI and digital sovereignty.

## 3. Information We Collect

### 3.1 Information We Do NOT Collect
- **Personal Files**: Your documents, images, and files are not collected by us
- **Conversation History**: AI conversations are not stored on our servers
- **Usage Analytics**: We do not track your usage patterns or behavior
- **Personal Identifiers**: We do not collect names, emails, or personal information

### 3.2 Information We May Collect
- **Error Reports**: Anonymous crash reports to improve software stability (optional)
- **Update Checks**: Version information to notify about updates
- **Open Source Contributions**: If you contribute code, your GitHub information becomes part of the public record

### 3.3 Local Data Storage
The Software stores data locally on your device:
- **Configuration Settings**: Your preferences and AI provider settings
- **API Keys**: Encrypted and stored locally for AI service access
- **Activity Logs**: Local logs for debugging and audit purposes (not transmitted)
- **Cache Data**: Temporary data to improve performance

## 4. How We Use Information

### 4.1 Local Processing
- All file operations are performed locally on your device
- AI processing happens through your chosen AI providers
- No personal data is sent to Rainy Cowork servers

### 4.2 Software Improvement
- Anonymous error reports help us fix bugs and improve stability
- Update notifications ensure you have the latest security patches
- Open-source contributions improve the software for everyone

## 5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

### 5.1 AI Service Providers
When you use AI features, data may be shared with:

**Enosis Labs Services:**
- Subject to [Enosis Labs Privacy Policy](https://enosislabs.vercel.app/privacy)
- Committed to responsible AI and data sovereignty
- Processes data according to their privacy standards

**Third-Party AI Providers:**
- **OpenAI**: Subject to [OpenAI Privacy Policy](https://openai.com/privacy/)
- **Google**: Subject to [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)
- **Groq**: Subject to [Groq Privacy Policy](https://groq.com/privacy-policy/)

### 5.2 No Data Selling
We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties.

### 5.3 Legal Requirements
We may disclose information if required by law, but given our local-first architecture, we have minimal data to disclose.

## 6. Data Security

### 6.1 Local Security Measures
- **Encryption**: API keys and sensitive data are encrypted at rest
- **Secure Storage**: Data is stored in protected local directories
- **Access Controls**: Only the Software can access its data files
- **Automatic Cleanup**: Temporary data is regularly cleaned

### 6.2 Network Security
- **HTTPS Only**: All network communications use encrypted connections
- **Certificate Validation**: We verify the authenticity of AI service endpoints
- **No Telemetry**: No usage data is transmitted to external servers

### 6.3 Open Source Security
- **Transparent Code**: All security measures are auditable in our open-source code
- **Community Review**: Security practices are reviewed by the open-source community
- **Regular Updates**: Security patches are released promptly

## 7. Your Privacy Rights

### 7.1 Data Control
You have complete control over your data:
- **Access**: All your data is stored locally and accessible to you
- **Modification**: You can modify or delete any local data
- **Portability**: Your data is stored in standard formats
- **Deletion**: Uninstalling the Software removes all local data

### 7.2 AI Provider Rights
For data processed by AI providers, you have rights under their respective privacy policies:
- Right to access your data
- Right to delete your data
- Right to data portability
- Right to opt-out of certain processing

### 7.3 Open Source Rights
As an open-source project, you have the right to:
- Audit our privacy practices through source code review
- Modify the Software to enhance privacy
- Contribute privacy improvements to the project

## 8. Children's Privacy

The Software is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are under 18, please obtain parental consent before using AI services that may have age restrictions.

## 9. International Users

### 9.1 Global Accessibility

### 9.2 Data Localization
Since processing is primarily local, your data generally remains in your jurisdiction unless you choose to use cloud-based AI services.

## 14. Privacy by Design

### 14.1 Core Principles
The Software is built with privacy by design:
- **Proactive**: Privacy protection is built-in, not added later
- **Default**: Privacy-friendly settings are the default
- **Embedded**: Privacy is embedded in the design and architecture

- **Minimal Permissions**: Requests only necessary system permissions
